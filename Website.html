Introduction

This project was created for IT Cybersecurity Awareness Week to help college students and staff recognise common online scams and learn practical prevention and reporting steps. Here we also provide multimedia content: text, graphics, audio and video — to deliver this key message:
Think Before You Click!

Common Types of Online Scams

Email Scams / Phishing

Fraudulent emails pretending to be from banks or services aiming to steal credentials.

Tip: Always check the sender's address and avoid clicking unknown links.

Fake Websites

Replica sites used to capture login or payment details.

Tip: Check URL spelling and HTTPS certificates.

Social Media Scams

Fake giveaways, impersonation or DMs asking for OTPs or money.

Tip: Never share your OTP with anyone.

Vishing & SMS Scams

Calls or SMS claiming to be banks or authorities requesting personal info.

Tip: Banks will never ask for OTP by phone or SMS.

Resources & How to Report

  • Contact your bank immediately if you suspect financial fraud.
  • Report phishing emails to your institution and relevant authorities.
  • Keep evidence such as screenshots, URLs and sender details.
